Most Iconic Grammy Fashion

Taking a look back in history-making moments through the decades

By: Cameron Allen

Published: April 6th 2025

1980s-1990s

1980s fashion was bold and flashy with bright colors and big silhouettes, while the 1990s embraced a more casual, minimalist vibe with grunge, denim, and simple styles.

1980- Debbie Harry wearing sequins at the 1980 Grammy Awards, leaving a pair of sunglasses balanced on her head.

1984- The King of Pop showed up to the ’84 Grammy Awards in a bedazzled military jacket and what would become his signature single glove.

1993- In one of the Grammys’ first-ever naked dresses, Celine Dion showed up to the 1993 show in a see-through black lace gown that showed off her black thong.

1998- In one of the most stunning red-carpet maternity dresses of all time, Jada Pinkett Smith wore a sheer sparkling gown to the 1998 Grammys.

2000-2009

The 2000s Grammy Awards showcased bold fashion, from Missy Elliot's iconic pink jump suit to Pop stars embraced shimmering gowns, while designer collaborations with Gucci and Chanel elevated red carpet style. The decade’s Grammy fashion was a mix of glamour, rebellion, and individuality. Here are a few looks highlighting the 2000s style!

2000- Britney Spears wearing an all-white gown inspired by Marilyn Monroe.

2001- Destiny Child wearing all green dresses designed by Tina Knowles.

2003- Missy Elliot wearing an iconic pink tracksuit that truly sums up 2000s style.

2006- Christina Aguilera wearing a two piece gown with belly chains. This type of look was scandalous at the time.

2010-2014

The 2010s Grammy Awards blended glamour with bold experimentation. Beyoncé and Taylor Swift stunned in elegant gowns, while Rihanna and Lady Gaga pushed fashion boundaries. Hip-hop stars like Kanye West and Cardi B embraced high-fashion streetwear, and menswear evolved with Pharrell and Harry Styles in bold, tailored looks. Here are a few memorable looks from the Grammy's in this era!

2010- Lady Gaga wearing a sclupture, futuristic-like gown designed by Armani Prive. This look sparked a lot of buzz online for its unconventional look.

2012- Nicki Minaj wearing a red silk robe created by Versace. This robe highlights an emblazoned Medusa head.

2014- Pharrell Williams wearing a brown tall hat that triggered many online memes.

2014- Queen Bey gave us a naked dress moment of her own in this sheer white floral gown by Michael Costello.

2015-2019

From 2015 to 2019, fashion blended 90s nostalgia with modern streetwear. Trends like crop tops, mom jeans, and chokers returned, while brands like Off-White and Yeezy pushed streetwear into the mainstream. Athleisure and gender-fluid styles rose in popularity, along with growing interest in sustainable fashion.

2015- Rihanna wore a Giambattista Valli Haute Couture gown, a strapless, empire-waist, tulle dress with a voluminous, bubblegum pink skirt.

2016- Taylor Swift wearing a bright two-piece gown created by Atelier Versace.

2017- Rihanna lit the red carpet on fire when stepped out in this two-piece Armani Privé look with Chopard jewelry.

2018- Wearing a sheer sequined Versace gown, SZA looked like an angel come to life as she walked the red carpet. Her dress featured dramatic tulle sleeves, a bodice made of sheer and intricate beading, and thigh-high slits.

2020-2025

The 2020–2025 Grammys blended classic elegance with bold innovation. Ariana Grande stunned in a voluminous Giambattista Valli gown, while Billie Eilish embraced oversized Gucci. Beyoncé wowed in black Schiaparelli, and Taylor Swift shined in Oscar de la Renta. Here are a few iconic looks from the Grammy's in this time period!

2020- Ariana Grande wearing a Cinderella-like gown by Giamabattista Valli.

2023- Harry Styles wearing a colorful geometric shapes one-piece in collaboration with Swarovski.

2024- Miley Cyrus wearing a custom Gold Maison Margiela gown made out of safety pins.

2025- Sabrina Carpenter wearing a custom pastel blue gown gown by JW Anderson featuring pearls and feathers.
